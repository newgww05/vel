Core Advantages

High-Absorbency Gel for
Heavily Exuding Wounds

Calcium Alginate Dressing is made from natural seaweed-derived calcium alginate fibres. On contact with wound exudate, the fibres undergo ion exchange — calcium ions in the fibre swap with sodium ions in the exudate, transforming the insoluble alginate into a soft, conformable sodium alginate gel. This process absorbs up to 20× its own weight in fluid, while releasing calcium ions that act as a co-factor in the coagulation cascade — actively supporting local haemostasis at the wound bed.

Available in flat pad and rope formats, the dressing is purpose-built for moderate-to-heavily exuding wounds, cavity wounds, sinuses and tunnels. As a primary contact dressing it absorbs exudate and supports haemostasis, then lifts off cleanly without adhering to fragile granulation tissue — requires a secondary cover dressing (foam or film) for fixation and barrier function.

⬡

Very High Exudate Absorption

The alginate fibre matrix absorbs up to 20× its own weight in wound exudate — significantly more than foam or hydrocolloid dressings. Vertical wicking locks fluid into the gel structure, preventing peri-wound maceration even in heavily exuding wounds.

◈

Haemostatic Calcium Release

The ion-exchange mechanism releases calcium ions (Ca²⁺) directly to the wound bed — a critical co-factor that supports prothrombin-to-thrombin conversion in the clotting cascade. Particularly valuable for surgical wounds, donor sites and minor-bleeding cavity wounds where local haemostasis matters.

◇

Gel Conformability & Cavity Filling

Available in flat pad and rope formats. The rope packs into deep cavities, sinuses and tunnels; once hydrated by exudate the gel conforms perfectly to the wound architecture, ensuring every recess receives moist healing protection.

○

Atraumatic Removal & Integrity

Once gelled, the dressing lifts off the wound bed without adhering to fragile new granulation tissue — change is virtually painless. The fibres maintain their structural integrity even when fully gelled, so the dressing can be removed in one piece without leaving residue in the wound.

Indications

Clinical Applications

Indicated as a primary contact dressing for moderate-to-heavily exuding chronic and acute wounds, particularly where haemostatic support or cavity-filling is required. Always cover with a secondary dressing (foam or film) for fixation and barrier protection.

Venous Leg Ulcers Pressure Injuries Stage III–IV Diabetic Foot Ulcers Cavity & Sinus Wounds Tunnelling Wounds Surgical Wounds with Bleeding Donor Sites Traumatic Wounds
Cautions
  • Requires a secondary cover dressing (foam or film) — alginate alone provides no barrier function and is non-adhesive.
  • Not suitable for dry wounds or eschar-covered necrotic tissue — without exudate the fibres cannot gel and may adhere to the wound bed.
  • For third-degree burns and heavily infected wounds, use only under the guidance of a healthcare professional. Irrigate the wound at each change to remove residual gel.
  • Discontinue immediately and seek medical advice if any abnormal reaction occurs.

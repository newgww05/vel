A U-Cut Made for the Hub — One Snug Seal Around Every External Catheter Connector.

The Velcura Transparent Film I.V. Dressing with Notch shares the same sterile, semi-permeable polyurethane film and picture-frame delivery system as the standard frame-style dressing — but adds a precisely-placed U-cut at one short edge. This U-notch hugs the catheter hub as it exits the dressing, eliminating the awkward "where does the hub go?" moment and creating a continuous, conformal seal right up to the connector.

That single design detail makes it the dressing of choice wherever the hub is bulky, low-profile or sits close to the skin: central venous catheters, PICC lines, implanted port access needles, and peripheral cannulas whose connector won't fit cleanly under a closed dressing. Everything else — the transparent window for continuous site visibility, the breathable PU film, the viral and bacterial barrier (in-vitro to 27 nm), the up-to-7-day wear time — is preserved without compromise.

U-Notch That Hugs the Hub

A precision U-cut at one short edge wraps neatly around the catheter hub as it exits the dressing — no gapping, no awkward tape patches, no compromise on the perimeter seal. Where standard center-cut dressings struggle, the notch makes the closure obvious.

Picture-Frame Delivery Carrier

The rigid paper frame holds the film flat and tension-free, allowing wrinkle-free one-handed placement in seconds. The notch is pre-oriented in the frame so nurses always know which edge meets the hub — placement is fast, repeatable, and forgiving.

Continuous Insertion-Site Visibility

The transparent PU window over the catheter entry point preserves uninterrupted visual surveillance for redness, swelling, leakage and migration — the same CDC/HICPAC-aligned monitoring afforded by any quality transparent dressing, now extended to bulky-hub access points.

Waterproof Viral & Bacterial Barrier

The semi-permeable PU film is impermeable to liquids, bacteria and viruses (in-vitro to 27 nm — the scale of hepatitis C) while remaining breathable to water vapour. Patients shower freely, pathogens stay out, and the skin beneath does not macerate.

Cautions
  • Orient the notch to face the catheter hub during application — re-positioning after adhesion will tear the seal at the U-cut edge.
  • Do not use on infected or heavily exuding wounds — the occlusive seal retains fluid and may promote anaerobic or fungal growth.
  • Do not stretch the film during application — tension creates blistering, edge lift, and shear at the notch.
  • Antimicrobial ointments containing polyethylene glycol (PEG) may degrade the PU film — avoid concurrent use under the dressing.
  • For thin, fragile, or steroid-affected skin, consider a silicone-bordered alternative; lift parallel to the skin at removal, not upward.
  • Single-use sterile device — do not resterilize by gamma, E-beam, or steam methods.

Core Advantages

Two Actions in One
Primary Dressing

Velcura Silver Alginate Dressing is a non-woven primary dressing spun from calcium alginate fibres — a natural polysaccharide derived from brown seaweed — uniformly impregnated with ionic silver. On contact with wound exudate, an ion exchange takes place between the calcium in the fibres and the sodium in the wound fluid: the fibres swell and convert into a soft, hydrophilic gel that absorbs up to twenty times its own weight in fluid, while silver ions are released slowly into the wound bed for continuous antimicrobial action.

This combination of high absorbency, gel conformability, intrinsic haemostasis and sustained broad-spectrum silver in a single fibre is what sets it apart from plain calcium alginate (no antimicrobial), silver foam sheets (cannot pack cavities) and silver hydrofiber (no calcium-driven haemostasis). It is indicated for infected or at-risk wounds with moderate to heavy exudate, including pressure injuries, diabetic foot ulcers, venous and arterial leg ulcers, surgical and post-debridement wounds, partial-thickness burns, and cavity, sinus and tunnelling wounds where the gel fills and conforms to dead space.

⬡

Dual-Action: Absorb & Kill

One fibre does the work of two dressings — high-absorbency alginate manages moderate to heavy exudate while controlled-release ionic silver simultaneously reduces bioburden, so a single primary dressing both drains and protects the infected wound.

◈

Sustained Broad-Spectrum Silver

Ionic silver is released gradually as exudate is absorbed, giving continuous antimicrobial activity for up to 7 days against a broad range of organisms — including MRSA and VRE — and remaining effective in the biofilm state, with no documented bacterial resistance to ionic silver.

◇

Gel-Forming, Cavity-Conforming

By ion exchange the dry fibres swell into a soft hydrophilic gel that absorbs up to 20× its weight, conforms intimately to irregular and cavity wounds, maintains a moist environment, and supports autolytic debridement — available in sheet and rope formats for packing sinuses and tunnels.

○

Haemostatic & Atraumatic

Calcium ions released from the fibres support the clotting cascade, making the dressing intrinsically haemostatic for bleeding and post-debridement wounds. The cohesive gel lifts cleanly and any residue rinses away with saline irrigation, for low-trauma, low-pain dressing changes.

Indications

Clinical Applications

For infected or at-risk wounds with moderate to heavy exudate, and for bleeding or freshly debrided wounds where haemostasis and exudate control are both needed. The conforming gel and rope format make it especially suitable for cavity, sinus and tunnelling wounds.

Infected Wounds Pressure Injuries Diabetic Foot Ulcers Venous & Arterial Leg Ulcers Cavity & Sinus Wounds Bleeding / Post-Debridement Surgical Wounds Partial-Thickness Burns
Cautions
  • Not suitable for dry, low-exudate, eschar-covered or necrotic wounds — alginate may desiccate the wound bed.
  • Not indicated for third-degree burns or wounds with exposed bone or tendon.
  • Avoid in patients with known sensitivity to silver or alginate. Requires a secondary dressing for fixation.
  • Review regularly; prolonged continuous silver use may rarely cause localised skin staining (argyria). Discontinue and seek advice if any abnormal reaction occurs.

See It, Seal It, Shower With It — The Island Dressing That Does All Three at Once.

Most wound-care decisions force a trade-off: a non-woven island dressing is absorbent but opaque, so the wound has to be uncovered every time it's checked, and it can't be showered. A pure transparent film keeps the wound visible and waterproof, but offers no exudate management. Velcura Film Island Dressing collapses both compromises into one product — a sterile, transparent polyurethane film border surrounding a central non-adherent absorbent pad. The patient walks into the shower; the clinician inspects the periwound area through the film; the wound bed itself heals quietly beneath a low-trauma contact layer that absorbs what it needs to absorb.

The PU film is more than a waterproof coating. It is a semi-permeable membrane: liquid water, bacteria, and viruses cannot pass inward, yet water vapour and oxygen pass freely outward — preventing the maceration that closed dressings produce while preserving a moist, infection-protected wound environment. Twelve size variants (6×7 to 15×20 cm) cover everything from small post-op incisions and IV puncture sites to large donor-site grafts. One construction; three properties at once.

Transparent PU Film Border

Clinicians see redness, swelling, leakage and edge migration through the clear film border without lifting the dressing — a level of periwound surveillance that opaque non-woven island dressings simply cannot offer. Fewer dressing changes, fewer occasions for contamination, lower MARSI exposure.

Waterproof, Breathable, Sealed

Patients can shower, wash and continue daily activity without disturbing the dressing. The semi-permeable PU film blocks liquids, bacteria and viruses inward while allowing water vapour outward — sealed against contamination, breathable against maceration.

Non-Adherent Absorbent Pad

A cotton/rayon absorbent core with a perforated PE wound-contact film handles low to moderate exudate without sticking to the wound bed. Granulation tissue lifts cleanly at change — no fibre shedding, no painful adhesion, no fresh injury at each dressing change.

All-in-One Island Construction

A single sterile application: self-adhesive PU border, central pad, ready-to-use. No separate primary dressing, no secondary tape, no add-on cover. One product handles the whole post-operative or superficial-wound cycle from theatre to discharge to home recovery.

Cautions
  • Not suitable for heavily exuding wounds — the central pad has a low-to-moderate absorption capacity. Choose a foam or absorbent fibre dressing for high-exudate wounds.
  • Not for clinically infected wounds — the dressing has no antimicrobial action. For at-risk or infected wounds, consider a silver-impregnated alternative.
  • Inspect the dressing daily; replace before the pad becomes saturated — strikethrough at the pad-film interface compromises the waterproof seal.
  • Do not stretch the film during application — tension trapped under the adhesive creates blisters, edge lift, and shear injury, especially on fragile skin.
  • Antimicrobial ointments containing polyethylene glycol (PEG) may degrade the PU film — avoid concurrent use under the dressing.
  • For severely fragile or steroid-affected skin, lift slowly and parallel to the skin (not upward), supporting the underlying tissue; consider a silicone-bordered alternative.
  • Single-use sterile device — do not resterilize by gamma, E-beam, or steam methods; discard if packaging is damaged or beyond expiry.

Stitches Without the Needle — When the Wound Doesn't Need What's Inside the Suture Tray.

Wound Closure Strips are the most elemental form of wound closure: thin, sterile strips of porous non-woven backing coated with a hypoallergenic acrylic adhesive. Applied directly across a clean, dry wound, they gently draw the edges together from the skin's surface — no needles, no staples, no occlusive glue. Their simplicity is their advantage: where a suture would puncture and a staple would compress, a strip simply holds. They are a standard closure technique in the emergency department, where over six million lacerations are treated each year in U.S. EDs alone (Pediatric Wound Care, NCBI 2025), and a routine option in clinics, first-aid stations and operating rooms (Clinical Gate, Methods of Wound Closure).

The strips excel precisely where percutaneous methods fall short — paediatric facial lacerations (no procedural anxiety, no needle), elderly thin or paper-thin skin where sutures "cheese-wire" through, ISTAP Category 1–2 skin tears, low-tension surgical incisions, and as adjunctive reinforcement after stitches or staples are removed. Porosity matters too: unlike skin adhesive (cyanoacrylate), the strips let the wound breathe, reducing maceration and supporting normal scab formation. Distinct from a surgical skin-closure strip, which is positioned as the surface layer over a subcuticular suture in a deeper-layer surgical closure, the Wound Closure Strip is used for primary closure on wounds that don't need a suture tray at all.

Truly Needleless Closure

Unlike sutures and staples, the strips never penetrate the dermis. There are no needle tracks, no railroad scars, no anxiety for paediatric patients, and no risk of needle-stick injury for the clinician. The closure is achieved entirely on the surface of intact skin.

Porous, Breathable Backing

Open non-woven structure permits free water-vapour escape and air exchange — a key advantage over cyanoacrylate skin glue, which forms an impermeable film. Reduced maceration, less risk of bacterial entrapment, and a more natural scab maturation under the strip.

Kind to Fragile Skin

The hypoallergenic acrylic adhesive lifts cleanly without tearing aged, atrophic, or steroid-thinned skin. Where sutures would "cheese-wire" through paper-thin elderly dermis, or staples would crush it, a strip simply rests on the surface — the closure of choice for the over-75s and skin-tear repair.

Primary Closure — Not Just an Adjunct

Unlike a surgical skin-closure strip (which serves as the surface layer over a deeper subcuticular suture), the Wound Closure Strip can close the wound entirely on its own — no underlying stitches required. Use as primary closure for superficial low-tension lacerations and skin tears; layer beneath skin adhesive to double tensile strength; or apply after early suture/staple removal to support the closure through remodelling. One product, three deployment patterns across the wound-healing timeline.

Cautions
  • Not suitable for high-tension wounds, gaping lacerations, or deep wounds requiring layered subcuticular closure — primary suturing is needed first.
  • Adhesion fails on damp, oily, hairy, or actively exuding skin — thoroughly clean and dry the periwound area before application; consider tincture of benzoin as an adhesive enhancer where appropriate.
  • Risk of Medical Adhesive-Related Skin Injury (MARSI) in elderly patients, those on long-term corticosteroids, or with compromised skin integrity — remove slowly and parallel to the skin, supporting the underlying tissue.
  • Do not use on clinically infected wounds or where evident debris, foreign bodies, or undermining cannot first be addressed.
  • Allow the strips to detach on their own as the skin sheds beneath them, or remove with adhesive-remover after 7–10 days; do not pull or peel forcibly.

Targeted Odour Control for Complex, Malodorous Wounds

Activated Charcoal Dressing Non-Adhesive features a knitted activated charcoal cloth enclosed in a non-adherent contact sleeve. The microporous carbon structure offers an exceptionally large surface area (over 1,000 m²/g) that physically adsorbs the volatile organic molecules — such as cadaverine, putrescine and organic acids — responsible for offensive wound odour, while simultaneously trapping bacteria and bacterial endotoxins from the exudate. A non-adherent contact surface ensures comfortable, atraumatic dressing changes for fragile peri-wound skin.

Purpose-designed for malodorous and infected chronic wounds: fungating tumour wounds, infected pressure injuries, diabetic foot ulcers with malodour, venous and arterial ulcers with bacterial colonisation, and any wound where odour or bioburden compromises patient dignity and clinical outcome. Used as a primary dressing or as a secondary layer over a contact-layer dressing.

Activated Charcoal Odour Adsorption

A high-purity activated charcoal cloth at the core physically traps the volatile odour molecules (cadaverine, putrescine, organic acids and other VOCs) generated by tissue degradation and bacterial colonisation. Unlike products that merely mask odour, the charcoal neutralises it at the source.

Bacterial Capture & Bioburden Control

The charcoal layer binds bacteria and bacterial endotoxins drawn into the dressing with exudate, helping to reduce surface bioburden and inflammatory load on the wound bed — particularly valuable for chronic contaminated and infected wounds.

Non-Adherent Wound Contact

The charcoal cloth is sealed within a soft non-adherent sleeve that does not stick to the wound bed or fragile peri-wound skin. Dressing changes are painless and atraumatic — no peeling forces on granulation tissue, no epidermal stripping (MARSI).

Versatile Use & Secondary Fixation

Use as a primary contact dressing on malodorous wounds, or layer over a contact-layer/alginate dressing for additional odour control. The non-adhesive design accepts any secondary fixation — bandage, tape, tubular netting or compression. Do not cut — cutting releases free charcoal particles into the wound bed.

Cautions
  • Do not cut. Cutting releases free activated charcoal particles into the wound bed, which may cause irritation and tattooing of tissue.
  • Requires a secondary fixation — bandage, tape, tubular netting or compression. The dressing is not self-adhesive.
  • Not suitable for dry wounds — moisten with saline or pair with a hydrogel/contact-layer if wound bed is dry.
  • Discontinue immediately and seek medical advice if any abnormal reaction occurs.

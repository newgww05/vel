A Second Skin for Superficial Wound Healing

Hydrocolloid Dressing (Super Thin) is engineered as an ultra-thin, near-invisible second skin for superficial wounds with light exudate. On contact with wound fluid, the hydrocolloid matrix (containing carboxymethylcellulose and other gel-forming particles) absorbs and gels — creating a sealed, moist micro-environment that accelerates epithelialisation, supports autolytic debridement of fibrinous tissue, and protects the wound from external contamination, water and friction. The thin, flexible film conforms to body contours so closely it disappears under clothing or on visible skin.

Especially indicated for superficial, lightly-exuding wounds where discretion and uninterrupted daily activity matter: Stage I–II pressure injuries on prominent points, superficial burns and donor sites, post-acne lesions, minor blisters, dry surgical incisions and the late maturation phase of healing wounds. Its thin profile makes it ideal for facial, hand and visible-site wounds where conventional dressings are too bulky.

Ultra-Thin Near-Invisible Profile

Less than 1 mm thick and semi-translucent, the dressing virtually disappears once applied — ideal for the face, hands, joints and any visible site where patients want to continue daily life without an obvious bandage. Flexible enough to follow body contours without lifting.

Autolytic Debridement & Moist Healing

On contact with exudate, the hydrocolloid matrix forms a soft gel that maintains the optimum moist environment for re-epithelialisation. The sealed micro-climate activates endogenous proteases, supporting gentle autolytic debridement of fibrin and necrotic tissue — no mechanical trauma to the wound bed.

Waterproof Protective Barrier

A semi-permeable polyurethane outer film is fully waterproof and blocks bacteria, viruses, dirt and friction — patients can shower, work and exercise without disturbing the dressing. Water vapour still escapes outward, so the skin doesn't macerate.

Self-Adhesive · 5-Day Wear Time

A gentle self-adhesive border secures the dressing without additional tape; the low-trauma adhesive lifts without epidermal stripping at change. Wear time of up to 5 days reduces dressing-change frequency, lowering disturbance to the wound and the burden on patients and caregivers.

Cautions
  • Not suitable for clinically infected wounds — the occlusive environment may encourage anaerobic bacterial growth.
  • Not recommended for heavily exuding wounds — the thin profile has limited absorbency; choose a foam or alginate dressing instead.
  • For deep, full-thickness or third-degree wounds, use only under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
  • Discontinue immediately and seek medical advice if any abnormal reaction occurs.

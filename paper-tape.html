Where Holding Tight Would Hurt — A Paper Tape Designed Around the Skin, Not the Dressing.

Most surgical tapes are designed around the dressing — how firmly it holds, how long it stays, how much exudate it tolerates. Paper Tape is designed around the patient. Where the skin is thin, fragile, or due to be exposed again at the next dressing change, the question stops being "how strong is the hold?" and becomes "how gentle is the lift?" Velcura Paper Tape is built for that question — a soft microporous paper backing with a hypoallergenic, latex-free acrylic adhesive that releases cleanly, leaves no residue, and minimises the epidermal stress that turns into Medical Adhesive-Related Skin Injury (MARSI).

Neonates have skin nearly 50% thinner than adults; the elderly often present with atrophic, steroid-affected or sun-damaged dermis; oncology and dermatology patients face repeated daily dressing changes. Across these populations, the published MARSI prevalence runs as high as 25%. Paper Tape is the clinical first-choice non-silicone option in exactly these scenarios — gentle enough to use day after day on the same square of skin, breathable enough to leave on without macerating, light enough to hold a gauze pad or IV gauze without overburdening the underlying tissue.

Microporous Paper Backing

Thousands of microscopic pores deliver the highest moisture-vapour transmission rate of any conventional medical tape — air and sweat pass freely, preventing the heat, maceration, and bacterial overgrowth that closed adhesive surfaces cultivate. Breathability is the structural property of the backing itself, not an engineered coating.

Designed for MARSI-Risk Skin

Calibrated for the populations most vulnerable to medical-adhesive-related skin injury: neonates and infants in NICU, frail elderly, dermatology and oncology patients, anyone on long-term corticosteroids, and patients in palliative care. The same calibration is also a comfort upgrade for any healthy adult.

Daily-Change Friendly Adhesion

Adheres securely on first application yet releases without epidermal stripping, fibre shedding, or sticky residue at change. Where another tape would punish the skin a little more each time it is replaced, paper tape stays kind through repeated cycles — the working tape of any unit that changes dressings daily.

Hand-Tearable, No Scissors Required

Tears cleanly across the grain with a single hand — no scissors, no jagged edges, no delay at the bedside. Equally easy to apply in the NICU, on a ward round, or in a home-care setting. Available in 1.25, 2.5 and 5 cm widths to suit anatomy from infant fingers to adult limbs.

Cautions
  • Limited tensile strength — not suitable as the sole fixation for heavy, bulky, or moisture-laden dressings; pair with a stronger tape where load-bearing matters.
  • Paper backing is moisture-sensitive — replace after bathing, swimming, sweat-saturation, or contact with wound exudate. Choose a transparent film tape for routinely wet environments.
  • Apply to clean, dry, intact skin — do not place the adhesive directly over open or infected wound beds.
  • Do not stretch during application — tension trapped under the tape creates blisters and shear injuries even on healthy skin, more so on fragile skin.
  • Remove slowly and parallel to the skin (not upward), supporting the underlying tissue with a free finger — the standard MARSI-prevention removal technique.
  • For the very highest-risk skin (extreme prematurity, epidermolysis bullosa, advanced steroid atrophy), a silicone-adhesive tape is the safer alternative to any acrylic-based product, paper tape included.
